Here we have an athlete preparing for a game. 这里，我们看到一位运动员正在为比赛做准备。⭐ prepare for：为……做准备
They've put on their gear and done their warmup, and now it's time for one more routine— stretching. 他已经穿戴好装备，完成了热身，现在还要进行最后一个固定环节，那就是拉伸。⭐ put on one's gear：穿戴装备｜warmup：热身｜it's time for…：到了……的时候｜routine：固定流程；常规步骤（B2）
Typically, athletes stretch before physical activity to avoid injuries like muscle strains and tears. 通常，运动员会在运动前进行拉伸，以避免肌肉拉伤、撕裂等损伤。⭐ physical activity：身体活动（B2）｜avoid injuries：避免受伤｜muscle strain：肌肉拉伤（C1）｜muscle tear：肌肉撕裂（C1）
But does stretching actually prevent these issues? 但拉伸真的能预防这些问题吗？⭐ prevent：预防；防止（B2）
And if so, how long do the benefits of stretching last? 如果可以，那么拉伸带来的益处能持续多久呢？⭐ if so：如果是这样的话（B2）｜the benefits of…：……的益处｜last：持续
To answer these questions, we need to know what's actually happening in the body when we stretch. 要回答这些问题，我们需要了解拉伸时身体里究竟发生了什么。⭐ to answer these questions：为了回答这些问题｜what's happening in the body：身体里发生了什么
There are two kinds of stretching our athlete might be doing. 这位运动员可能会做两种类型的拉伸。
Dynamic stretches, which are controlled movements that engage a range of muscles throughout the motion. 第一种是动态拉伸，它是指受控制的动作，在整个动作过程中会调动多组肌肉。⭐ dynamic stretch：动态拉伸（C1）｜controlled movement：受控制的动作｜engage a range of muscles：调动多组肌肉（C1）｜throughout the motion：在整个动作过程中
And static stretches, where the stretcher holds a position to keep specific muscles at a fixed length and tension. 第二种是静态拉伸，即保持一个姿势，使特定肌肉维持在固定的长度和张力下。⭐ static stretch：静态拉伸（C1）｜hold a position：保持一个姿势｜fixed length and tension：固定的长度和张力（C1）
Our athlete is currently doing a static hamstring stretch— a 30 second interval where he pulls his hamstring beyond its usual range of motion. 此时，这位运动员正在做一次30秒的静态腿后肌拉伸，把腿后肌拉伸到超过平时活动范围的位置。⭐ hamstring stretch：腿后肌拉伸（C1）｜interval：持续时间；间隔（B2）｜beyond its usual range of motion：超出其正常活动范围（C1）｜range of motion：活动范围（C1）
However, while we often think of stretching a muscle like stretching a rubber band, muscles are actually comprised of various tissue types, which interact to make a complex material. 然而，虽然我们常把拉伸肌肉比作拉橡皮筋，但肌肉实际上由多种不同类型的组织构成，这些组织相互作用，形成一种复杂的材料。⭐ think of… as…：把……看作……｜be comprised of：由……组成（C1）｜tissue type：组织类型｜interact：相互作用（B2）
Rubber is elastic, meaning it resists being stretched and then returns to its original shape. 橡胶具有弹性，也就是说，它会抵抗拉伸，并最终恢复原来的形状。⭐ elastic：有弹性的（B2）｜resist being stretched：抵抗被拉伸（C1）｜return to its original shape：恢复原状
But muscle is viscoelastic. 但肌肉属于黏弹性材料。⭐ viscoelastic：黏弹性的（C2）
This means that, in addition to those elastic qualities, muscle changes under the stress of being stretched. 这意味着，除了具有弹性之外，肌肉在受到拉伸应力时本身也会发生变化。⭐ in addition to：除……之外（B2）｜under the stress of…：在……应力作用下（C1）｜elastic qualities：弹性特性
Let's see this in action. 让我们看看这一过程是如何实际发生的。⭐ see… in action：看看……实际运作的样子（C1）
At the large scale, this hamstring stretch is pulling on layers of protective tissue surrounding the muscle fibers, as well as the tendons that connect this muscle to nearby bones. 从宏观层面来看，这次腿后肌拉伸会牵拉包裹肌纤维的多层保护组织，以及连接肌肉和附近骨骼的肌腱。⭐ at the large scale：从宏观层面来看（C1）｜pull on：牵拉｜protective tissue：保护组织｜muscle fiber：肌纤维｜tendon：肌腱（C1）
These tissues contain elastic proteins like collagen and elastin, which allow them to slightly elongate over the duration of the stretch. 这些组织中含有胶原蛋白和弹性蛋白等弹性蛋白质，使它们能够在拉伸过程中略微延长。⭐ contain：含有｜collagen：胶原蛋白（C1）｜elastin：弹性蛋白（C2）｜elongate：拉长；延长（C1）｜over the duration of…：在……持续期间（C1）
At the smaller scale, these skeletal muscle fibers are comprised of millions of sarcomeres— the smallest contracting unit of muscle tissue. 从更微观的层面来看，这些骨骼肌纤维由数百万个肌小节组成，而肌小节是肌肉组织最小的收缩单位。⭐ at the smaller scale：从微观层面来看（C1）｜skeletal muscle fibers：骨骼肌纤维（C1）｜be comprised of：由……组成（C1）｜sarcomere：肌小节（C2）｜contracting unit：收缩单位（C2）
Sarcomere's long, fibrous proteins can relax to elongate muscle fibers or they can contract to shorten them— pulling on tendons and protective tissue to create the force propelling our athlete's body. 肌小节中细长的纤维蛋白可以放松，使肌纤维变长；也可以收缩，使肌纤维缩短，从而牵拉肌腱和保护组织，产生推动运动员身体运动的力量。⭐ fibrous proteins：纤维蛋白（C1）｜elongate muscle fibers：拉长肌纤维（C1）｜contract：收缩（B2）｜pull on：牵拉｜propel：推动；驱动（C1）
And when those tissues have been stretched out, they retain their strength at longer lengths, allowing our athlete to take full advantage of his improved range of motion. 当这些组织被拉长后，它们能够在更长的长度下保持力量，从而使运动员能够充分利用自己增加的活动范围。⭐ stretch out：拉长；伸展开｜retain their strength：保持力量（C1）｜take full advantage of：充分利用（B2）｜improved range of motion：提升后的活动范围（C1）
Unlike a rubber band, this muscle's resistance to stretching decreases with each 30 second stretch, allowing our athlete to continually elongate his hamstring. 与橡皮筋不同，肌肉对拉伸的阻力会随着每一次30秒的拉伸而减小，因此运动员可以不断把腿后肌拉得更长。⭐ unlike：不同于｜resistance to：对……的阻力（B2）｜decrease with：随着……而减少｜continually：持续地（B2）｜elongate：拉长（C1）
And this improved flexibility likely decreases the chance of incurring certain muscle injuries. 这种柔韧性的提升，很可能会降低发生某些肌肉损伤的风险。⭐ improved flexibility：提高的柔韧性｜decrease the chance of：降低……的可能性｜incur an injury：遭受、发生损伤（C1）
But due to muscle's elastic properties, this effect will be gone in just 10 minutes without further activity. 但由于肌肉具有弹性，这种效果如果没有进一步活动，仅仅10分钟后就会消失。⭐ due to：由于（B2）｜elastic properties：弹性特性（C1）｜further activity：后续活动
The exercise from his match should elevate his muscle's temperature and continue elongating the muscle, maintaining his increased flexibility throughout the game. 比赛中的运动会提高肌肉温度，并持续拉长肌肉，从而使增加的柔韧性在整场比赛中得以维持。⭐ elevate muscle temperature：提高肌肉温度（C1）｜maintain：维持（B2）｜throughout the game：整场比赛期间
But once he gives his hamstrings a break, they'll retract back to their previous state. 但一旦腿后肌得到休息，它们就会回缩到之前的状态。⭐ give… a break：让……休息｜retract：回缩；缩回（C1）｜previous state：之前的状态
So if all this is just temporary, how can you permanently improve your flexibility? 那么，如果这一切都只是暂时的，又怎样才能永久提高柔韧性呢？⭐ temporary：暂时的（B2）｜permanently：永久地（B2）｜improve flexibility：提高柔韧性
For skeletal muscles, improved flexibility comes from additional sarcomeres, which allow you to maintain strength at even greater lengths. 对于骨骼肌来说，柔韧性的提升来自于新增的肌小节，它们使你能够在更长的肌肉长度下仍然保持力量。⭐ come from：来源于｜additional sarcomeres：新增的肌小节（C2）｜maintain strength：保持力量｜at greater lengths：在更长的长度下（C1）
Sarcomeres are added and subtracted to muscles depending on how frequently they're used, so improving overall flexibility requires a comprehensive stretching regimen. 肌小节会根据肌肉使用的频率不断增加或减少，因此，提高整体柔韧性需要一套系统、全面的拉伸计划。⭐ depending on：取决于｜how frequently：使用频率｜overall flexibility：整体柔韧性｜comprehensive：全面的；系统的（C1）｜stretching regimen：拉伸训练方案（C1）
Plus, you need to stretch often— very often. 此外，你需要经常拉伸，而且是非常经常。⭐ plus：此外（口语，B2）
Current research suggests at least 10 minutes a day for roughly two months. 目前的研究建议，每天至少拉伸10分钟，坚持大约两个月。⭐ current research suggests…：当前研究表明……（B2）｜roughly：大约（B2）
And since it's unsafe to hold any stretch for too long, it's recommended that you break up your stretching within any given session. 由于长时间保持任何一种拉伸姿势都不安全，因此建议在每一次训练中把拉伸时间分成多个小段进行。⭐ hold a stretch：保持拉伸姿势（C1）｜it's recommended that…：建议……（B2）｜break up：分开；拆分（phrasal verb）｜within any given session：在任何一次训练过程中（C1）
Frequent stretching also makes lasting changes to your brain. 频繁拉伸还会对你的大脑产生持久性的改变。⭐ frequent stretching：频繁拉伸｜lasting changes：持久性的变化（B2）
The more you stretch a muscle, the higher your pain threshold becomes, allowing you to stretch further and further. 你拉伸肌肉越多，你的疼痛耐受阈值就会越高，从而使你能够拉得越来越远。⭐ pain threshold：疼痛阈值（C1）｜allow sb to do sth：使某人能够……｜further and further：越来越远
Long-term improvements to your flexibility can provide a better range of motion for your joints, potentially reducing your risk of muscle injury in the short and long-term. 柔韧性的长期提升能够让关节拥有更大的活动范围，并有可能降低你在短期和长期发生肌肉损伤的风险。⭐ long-term improvements：长期改善（B2）｜range of motion：活动范围（C1）｜potentially：有可能地（B2）｜reduce your risk of：降低……的风险（B2）｜in the short and long-term：从短期和长期来看（C1）
But it's also possible to stretch too much, as flexible people can move their joints in potentially dangerous ways. 不过，拉伸也可能过度，因为柔韧性很好的人可能会让关节活动到存在危险的角度。⭐ stretch too much：拉伸过度｜potentially dangerous：具有潜在危险的（B2）｜move one's joints：活动关节
Stretching also isn't helpful for every kind of movement, especially those where muscle injuries aren't very common. 拉伸也并非对所有运动都有帮助，尤其是那些本身肌肉损伤并不常见的运动。⭐ be helpful for：对……有帮助｜every kind of：每一种……｜muscle injuries aren't very common：肌肉损伤并不常见

When it comes down to it, different lifestyles require different kinds of mobility. 归根结底，不同的生活方式需要不同类型的身体活动能力。⭐ when it comes down to it：归根结底；说到底（C1，口语高频）｜lifestyle：生活方式（B2）｜mobility：活动能力；身体灵活性（C1）
